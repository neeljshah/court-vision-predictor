What is CourtVision

CourtVision is an NBA prop-betting analytics platform. It scrapes lines from ~10 sportsbooks, grades each prop against an in-house quantile-regression model, and ranks the best bets by expected value. The site has three pages:

  • 1 / — tonight's games. One card per matchup.
  • 2 /tonight — clicking a game opens its bet board: projected box score on top, single bets grouped into S/A/B/C tiers below.
  • 3 /parlays — auto-generated same-book parlays (every leg placeable at one sportsbook). 2–4 legs, ranked by EV.
How the model works

Seven player-prop stats (PTS, REB, AST, FG3M, STL, BLK, TOV) each have their own ensemble of XGBoost/LightGBM/MLP models trained on rolling form, opponent defense, pace, rest, and CV-derived behavioral signals. Predictions use quantile-50 (q50) regression — the model targets the median outcome, which is what the sportsbook's O/U line is set against. Walk-forward CV over two NBA seasons gates every change.

Per-stat sigma is calibrated against 50K out-of-fold rows so the model_prob on a bet card matches the empirical residual distribution. During the playoffs sigma is widened ×1.20 (literature, no playoff OOF data) — you'll see a purple PLAYOFFS · σ × 1.2 badge in the header when this applies.

Reading a bet card
q50
Median model projection for the stat. If q50 ≥ line, model takes the OVER; else UNDER.
edge
q50 − line, in stat units (e.g. "−1.3 blocks").
model
Probability the bet hits, derived from q50 and per-stat sigma. Capped at 85% — no honest single-prop model is more certain than that.
market
De-vig implied probability at the posted price. The benchmark for what the market thinks.
gap
model − market, in percentage points. The honest edge — gap of +10pp means model expects this to hit 10pp more often than the market is pricing.
EV %
Expected return per $100 staked at the best posted price. EV = model × payout − (1 − model) × 100.
Kelly
Suggested stake at $100 bankroll. Quarter-Kelly capped at 4% ($4 max per single bet) for variance control.
tier (S/A/B/C)
EV bucket: S ≥ 15%, A 8–15%, B 3–8%, C < 3%. Bets are grouped by tier on the per-game view.
Projected box score

Opening a game card lands you on /tonight filtered to that matchup, with a projected per-player box score at the top. Pregame, each cell shows the model's projection of that player's typical performance (the same number we use to grade O/U prop bets — the prop line is set at "typical"). The team total shown next to each team is the model's projected team score, directly comparable to a sportsbook team total line.

Once the game tips off and a live boxscore feed is available, the cells flip to live values — current stats and an end-of-game projection from the full live engine (residual heads, foul trouble, blowout, heat-check, learned Q4 minutes). The panel polls every 30 seconds.

A live win probability bar appears once the game reaches end of Q3 (the earlier-quarter boosters didn't pass our Brier-score validation gate, so we suppress them to avoid misleading numbers).

Parlays

The /parlays page auto-generates ranked combinations. Three rules are hard-coded:

  • 1 Same book per slip. Every leg in a given parlay can be placed at one sportsbook. No cross-book legs.
  • 2 2–4 legs. Engine ranks by Monte-Carlo EV; 3-leggers tend to dominate the top of the list.
  • 3 Per-book filter. Toggle which books you have via the chip strip — only those parlays render. Setting is shared with /tonight via local storage.
Sportsbook coverage

Scraped books: DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, BetRivers, ESPN BET, PointsBet, Pinnacle, Hard Rock Bet, Fanatics, Bet365, PrizePicks, Underdog, BetOnline.

Bovada is excluded from consolidation — their lines post late and stay wide relative to sharp books, distorting consensus.

Honest gaps

EV magnitudes can look high. Per-stat sigma is calibrated against the OOF residual distribution, but the model has a known structural UNDER lean — q50 sits below the line on most picks. The EV magnitudes you see are arithmetically correct given the q50 and sigma, but they assume the q50 itself is unbiased.

Playoff calibration is heuristic. The OOF dataset is regular-season only, so the ×1.20 playoff sigma boost is from literature, not from your data.

Parlay EVs compound any per-leg miscalibration. Treat parlay EV as a ranking signal, not a literal expected return.
